# AO Optimization Ledger

This ledger inventories every optimization or diagnostic requested for the UVSR
AO performance project, every relevant Activision GTAO and Intel XeGTAO
technique, and additional candidates found in primary-source research. It is
anchored to canonical commit `5f43205ecfe00e31fd64af34cad0f031472a224c` and
the target profile of 1920x1080 output, half-linear-resolution AO, eight total
radial samples, radius 3.0, thickness 0.5, quadratic spacing, AO only, and no
adaptive, temporal, or user-enabled spatial filtering.

## Ledger Status

All 413 candidates record a current allowed disposition based on the current
code, primary-source inspection, and the zero-inactive-cost contract. The
totals are
64 `Already equivalent`, 52 `Implemented and retained`, 51 `Implemented as
optional experiment`, 17 `Implemented as benchmark-only control`, 50 `Not
applicable`, 92 `Blocked by architecture`, and 87 `Blocked by correctness`.
No row is labeled `Rejected after measurement` because no qualifying controlled
target run has rejected a candidate, and no row is labeled `Blocked by
licensing` because the inspected source licenses did not create such a block.

Results that cite source inspection are established on `5f43205`; performance
results remain pending unless an adapter, build, profile key, warm-up, sample
count, median, and p95 are recorded. No cited timing from another product or GPU
is a UVSR result.

Blocked dispositions are not measured rejections. In particular, M-010,
A-004, F-030, and O-039 remain unmet deliverable/evidence gaps, and the
isolatable shader experiments D-023, D-024, D-025, D-026, R-025, L-015, and
O-022 have no demonstrated correctness, API, licensing, or zero-cost-off
barrier. Their current architecture dispositions mean only that
the build has no complete selectable implementation; that absence does not
establish that a benchmark-only PSO would be unreasonable. D-019, D-020,
L-016, and O-037 have concrete prerequisites recorded in their rows, but still
need implementation or stronger generated-code/tooling evidence before they
can be called evaluated experiments. These entries are intentionally visible
as incomplete requirements rather than being reported as slower, rejected, or
impossible.

## Classification and Evidence Conventions

- Exact means the same input texels and LODs, sample set and ownership order,
  estimator, weights, output semantics, and required intermediate quantization
  are preserved. Generated-code changes still require image and fixture checks.
- Numerical means the algorithm is retained but arithmetic, transcendental
  evaluation, precision, format, or rounding changes.
- Algorithmic means sampling, depth representation, reconstruction, history,
  filtering, visibility, or lighting behavior changes.
- Diagnostic means a deliberately non-product workload used to isolate a timing
  floor. Diagnostic controls never establish product-image performance.
- Zero inactive cost means CPU selection of the reference pipeline with no hot
  shader branch, extra binding, texture, pass, history, or optional dispatch.
- Expected-impact entries are engineering estimates, not measurements. They use
  the supplied approximately 1.2 ms trace, 1.1 ms filtering, and 0.9 ms Other
  observations; exact logical byte or invocation changes where derivable; and
  low/medium/high ranking elsewhere. Estimates overlap heavily and must not be
  added together. Only a recorded adapter, build, profile key, warm-up, sample
  count, median, and p95 can replace an estimate with a result.

family. It uses the user's 2.5-2.7 ms complete AO baseline and the 2.6 ms
midpoint for percentages. Positive values are possible savings; negative values
are possible regressions. These are engineering forecasts, not measured target-
GPU results. Ranges overlap and are non-additive because fixed specialization,
bindings, packed noise, reconstruction, and fusion often remove the same work.
Rank reflects plausible impact and maximum credible upside, not a claim that
each range has a positive midpoint.
Retention still requires isolated Release runs on the target Intel Core Ultra 9
185H/Xe-LPG adapter, 120 warm-up frames, at least 240 complete measured frames,

## Source Integration Boundaries

### Activision Closest-Match Boundary

The current build implements two CPU-selected benchmark controls: the
same-engine eight-read analytic horizon path and the twelve-read horizon path
with the documented Activision 4x4 spatial, six-phase temporal, and four-phase
radial schedule. `src/visibility_performance_plan.cpp` deliberately marks both
as partial controls. The build does not implement Activision's coupled
half-depth reduction, derivative-guided 4x4 reconstruction, temporal EMA and
disocclusion rules, screen-space horizon-thickness EMA, far fade, or
depth-derived-normal pipeline. Those omissions govern A-004, A-005, A-009
through A-012, and A-014.

Each missing stage could in principle live behind a separate CPU-selected PSO
and conditional resource, so inactive-path cost is not the blocker: Reference
already selects the canonical UVSR PSOs and allocates none of those resources.
The current block is architectural completeness and correctness. The depth,
filter, and temporal stages form one coupled estimator pipeline; exposing the
trace-only subset under a full "PS4 closest match" label would produce a
mis-scoped comparison. Evidence is the `PartialBenchmarkControl` status and
implementation notes in `src/visibility_performance_plan.cpp`, the absence of
those entry points in `src/shaders.cfg`, and the retained legacy resolve,
temporal, and composition selection in `src/screen_space_visibility.cpp`.

### XeGTAO Integration Block

`XeGtaoClosestMatch` is explicitly `Unavailable` in
`src/visibility_performance_plan.cpp`. The current horizon shader is not a full
XeGTAO integration: it lacks the 64x64 Hilbert plus R2 schedule, multi-slice
quality family, XeGTAO prefiltered depth mips and reduction, cosine-space
horizon interpolation and falloffs as one matched estimator, packed-edge
denoiser pass family, generated-normal modes, 1.5-range R8 encoding, and mixed
precision. This boundary governs M-010 and X-002 through X-012, X-017, and
X-019 through X-023; correctness-gated product transfers remain separate rows.

Reference could preserve zero inactive cost for a complete XeGTAO pipeline, so
inactive overhead is again not the reason for omission. A matched control needs
its own depth, normal, trace, denoise, and application resource/pass contract;
none exists in the current renderer or `src/shaders.cfg`. Calling the partial
analytic horizon shader XeGTAO would be factually and experimentally incorrect.
The explicit unavailable status, missing shader entry points, and retained
legacy resource selection are the current-build evidence. The code keeps the
profile visible as unavailable instead of silently substituting the partial
control.

## Profiling and Timing Evidence

The target adapter is Intel Core Ultra 9 185H integrated Arc/Xe-LPG. Intel's
[product specification](https://www.intel.com/content/www/us/en/products/sku/236849/intel-core-ultra-9-processor-185h-24m-cache-up-to-5-10-ghz/specifications.html)
lists eight Xe cores, shared-memory integrated graphics, and DirectX 12.2;
Intel identifies Core Ultra Series 1 graphics as
[Xe-LPG](https://www.intel.com/content/www/us/en/support/articles/000098346/graphics.html).
No public Xe-LPG-specific optimization guide was found, so Xe-HPG guidance is
architecturally adjacent evidence only and every performance claim remains
target-run dependent.

[PIX hardware counters](https://devblogs.microsoft.com/pix/hardware-counters-in-gpu-captures/)
can support instruction, utilization, cache, barrier, and traffic evidence.
[PIX GPU-capture guidance](https://devblogs.microsoft.com/pix/gpu-captures/)
warns that replay does not preserve cross-queue overlap, so async candidates
must use a Timing Capture and complete-frame measurement. Intel's
[GPA overview](https://www.intel.com/content/www/us/en/developer/tools/graphics-performance-analyzers/overview.html)
states that 2025.1 was the final release and the product was discontinued in
2026; therefore maintained GPA availability cannot block completion. Available
Intel or PIX counters should include EU activity/stalls, sampler busy/stalls,
cache behavior, bandwidth, occupancy/registers, divergence, barriers, and UAV
traffic where the driver exposes them.

External timing scope must stay explicit:

- Activision slide 89 reports a 1080p half-resolution PS4 trace at 0.35 ms and
  spatial/temporal denoiser at 0.15 ms; it does not separately label pyramid,
  full-resolution application, or lighting composition.
- The visibility paper's Table 1 is a sampling-pass comparison on RTX 2080;
  its roughly 0.3 ms denoiser is excluded. The reported 8, 12, and 16 counts are
  per side, so physical depth reads are twice those labels.
- XeGTAO README timings cover its stated prefilter/main/denoise scope and
  settings, not UVSR's full composition path.
- SAO timings include their stated guard band and depth hierarchy. A hierarchy
  is retained in UVSR only when construction plus trace is a net win or a
  semantically compatible producer is genuinely shared.

## Remaining Validation

The inventory, expected-impact ranking, final dispositions, and explicit
Activision/XeGTAO integration boundaries are complete for the current build.
Implemented rows identify their shader/profile or CPU resource-plan evidence;
blocked rows identify the missing correctness proof or complete selectable
pipeline contract; and all optional paths preserve the Reference zero-cost
contract by design.

Controlled Intel Core Ultra 9 185H timings and final image acceptance remain a
user-run validation step. Until a result records the adapter, Release build,
exact profile/permutation key, 120-frame warm-up, at least 240 complete frames,
median, p95, and quality evidence, every impact entry remains an estimate and no
target-GPU improvement is claimed. A later measured rejection may replace a
current experimental or blocked disposition, but an external product timing or
unmatched scope cannot do so.

Local DXC compilation and reflection can establish that an entry point exists,
that compile-time macros remove code, and that advanced layouts match reflected
resource subsets. It does not establish Intel ISA instruction count, register
allocation, occupancy, EU activity, sampler pressure, cache hit rate, or target
bandwidth. Those counter fields, the before/after stage table, and reference-
versus-candidate image acceptance remain deliberately unfilled until the final
Release smoke/capture evidence and user-led target run exist.
